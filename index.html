About Me

Ratan Naval Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons1. He was the chairman of the Tata Group from 1990 to 2012, and served as interim chairman from October 2016 through February 20171. He continues to head its charitable trusts1. Ratan Tata was born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, into a Parsi Zoroastrian family1. He is the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted by Ratanji Tata, son of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group1. He graduated from Cornell University College of Architecture with a bachelor's degree in architecture1. He joined Tata in 1961, where he worked on the shop floor of Tata Steel1. He later succeeded J. R. D. Tata as chairman of Tata Sons upon the latter's retirement in 19911
